The benefits and risks regarding of internet use in preventing phishing and identity theft

The prominence in online safety have never been more prominent as the rising numbers of internet use becomes more widespread. With 5.35 billion users across the globe the increasing reliance for everyday activities has never been more obvious. The introduction of the internet have transformed the way we perform daily tasks such as communication banking and sharing information. Though the benefits are numerous its unparalled convenience brings however a host of challenges especially in the realm of identity theft and phishing.

Benefits & Drawbacks

How to prevent phishing and identity theft

The internet does offer a selection of resources that aid in the prevention of phishing and identity theft. One example can be the many browsers that can be used, such as Google and Microsoft Edge, both use anti-phishing tools that prevent phishing scams and identity theft.Furthermore, online websites do offer educational material to assist users in recognizing and avoiding common phishing tactics.However, with major technological advancements, cybercriminals have made it easier to do phishing attacks and identity theft by creating fake websites that look extremely identical to legitimate websites. Additionally, hackers have started to use AI to create more convincing phishing emails. because of these two reasons and many more there has now been a 61% increase in reported phishing scams. Though websites and companies are starting to create ways to cease phishing scams and identity theft, it does start to raise some ethical questions such as how personal data is being treated. We selected this topic as numerous victims of phishing and identity theft have risen to the technological advancements of the internet, and its benefits are starting to be outweighed by the advancement of scammers. It is a major issue that will still be relevant for years to come.
